# Stakeholder Analysis: AI BuryatMyasoprom Digital Transformation

## Key Stakeholder Groups

### Executive Leadership
- **General Director**: Primary decision-maker, focused on ROI and competitive advantage
- **Finance Director**: Concerned with budget, cost reduction, and financial metrics
- **Operations Director**: Interested in process efficiency and quality improvement

### Operational Teams
- **Export Department**: Heavy users of customs documentation system
- **Quality Control**: Primary beneficiaries of traceability and monitoring systems
- **Logistics Team**: Users of cold chain monitoring and shipment tracking
- **IT Department**: Technical implementation and system maintenance

### External Stakeholders
- **Chinese Importers**: Require compliance documentation and quality certificates
- **Customs Authorities**: Both Russian and Chinese - regulatory compliance
- **Suppliers**: Local farms providing raw materials for traceability system
- **Technology Vendors**: API providers for customs, logistics, and payments

## Stakeholder Matrix

| Stakeholder | Influence | Interest | Current Attitude | Engagement Strategy |
|-------------|-----------|----------|------------------|-------------------|
| General Director | High | High | Champion | Regular strategy sessions |
| Export Manager | Medium | High | Resistant | Training & incremental rollout |
| Chinese Partners | Medium | Medium | Supportive | API access & transparency |
| Customs Officials | High | Low | Neutral | Compliance-first approach |

## Communication Plan
- **Weekly**: Project updates to executive team
- **Bi-weekly**: Operational team training sessions
- **Monthly**: External stakeholder alignment meetings
- **Quarterly**: System demo for Chinese partners

## Success Criteria by Stakeholder
- **Executives**: ROI achievement, market share growth
- **Operational Teams**: Time savings, error reduction
- **Chinese Partners**: Faster clearance, better visibility
- **Regulators**: 100% compliance, accurate documentation
